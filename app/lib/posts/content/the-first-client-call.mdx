## Why the First Call Decides the Deal

By the time you send a quote, the decision is usually already made. The first call is where a prospect decides whether you are a vendor they manage or a partner they trust. Everything after that is paperwork.

That is why the call deserves a structure. Winging it works until you are busy, tired, or talking to someone sharper than your last three clients.

## Prepare in Five Minutes

Preparation is not a research project. It is five minutes that signals you take them seriously.

1. Look at their current listings and note one thing you genuinely like.
2. Check how their photos look today, so you know what you are improving.
3. Have your three packages open, so pricing is instant if they ask.

## Open by Setting the Agenda

Start by telling them how the call will go. "I would love to hear about your business first, then I will share how we usually help, and if it is a fit we can talk timing." It takes ten seconds and it hands you quiet control of the conversation.

A prospect who knows the shape of the call relaxes, and a relaxed prospect tells you the truth.

## Diagnose Before You Pitch

The instinct on a sales call is to talk. Resist it. The more they describe the problem, the more precisely you can describe the solution.

### Ask About the Last Launch

Ask what their last launch felt like, where their listings stall, what they wish their current photographer did differently. Every answer is ammunition for a pitch that sounds like it was written for them, because it was.

### Earn the Right to Pitch

Only once you understand the problem do you earn the right to describe the solution. Pitch too early and you are guessing in public.

## Summarize and Confirm Next Steps

Before you hang up, play it back. Restate what they told you, name the package that fits, and agree on one concrete next step with a date attached. Vague endings produce vague follow-ups.

  A call that ends with "I will send some info over" is a call you will be chasing for a month. End
  with a date instead.
</Quote>

## After the Call

Send the recap within the hour, while you are still fresh in their mind. Three sentences: what you heard, what you recommend, what happens next. The studio that follows up first and clearest almost always wins the booking.
